This reflection tree is designed to guide employees through a structured end-of-day evaluation across three psychological dimensions: locus of control, contribution orientation, and radius of concern.

The first axis (Locus: Victim vs Victor) evaluates whether individuals take ownership of their actions or attribute outcomes to external factors. This is based on the concept of Locus of Control (Rotter, 1954) and Growth Mindset (Dweck, 2006).

The second axis (Contribution vs Entitlement) focuses on whether individuals reflect on what they contributed versus what they expected to receive. This draws from Organizational Citizenship Behavior (Organ, 1988) and psychological entitlement theory.

The third axis (Self-Centrism vs Allocentrism) expands the individual’s perspective from self-focused thinking to considering team and customer impact. This is inspired by self-transcendence (Maslow, 1969) and perspective-taking research.

The branching logic ensures that each response leads to a relevant follow-up question and reflection, creating a deterministic and structured experience. Reflection nodes provide insight without judgment, encouraging self-awareness and growth.

Trade-offs included limiting the number of questions per axis to maintain usability while ensuring coverage of all dimensions. With more time, the tree could be expanded with deeper branching and personalized summaries.
